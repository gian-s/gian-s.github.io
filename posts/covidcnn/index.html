Convolutional Neural Networks to Detect COVID-19

Fine tuning large scaled CNNs for classifying x-rays of lungs with COVID-19
December 2020 · Boston University | Suggest Changes
Table of Contents
  • Summary
  • Role
  • Results

Summary#

Final project during Introduction to Artificial Intelligence course at Boston University CS440. I was tasked with creating a classification system for x-ray images of lungs that were infected with Covid-19. I read through different research papers and implementations of architectures that other researchers have worked on and used their work as a basis for the experiments I conducted on my project.

Role#

During the project, I read various research papers on different Convolutional Neural Network Architectures that were being used for medical imaging classification. With the use of transfer learning, I was able to use larger pre-trained CNNs in order to create a classifier to detect if the X-ray had Covid, or did not. I also trained a multi-class CNN in order to classify between Normal, COVID-19, Viral and Bacterial Pneumonia. I used Tensorflow to create the Neural Networks, and various python packages to create loss/accuracy graphs and a t-Sne plot of the penultimate layer in the network to attempt to visualize how well the classifier did.

Results#

The results and visualization of the project can be viewed in the Final Report that was submitted at the end of the class which earned me a letter grade of A.
